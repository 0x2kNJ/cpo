# Domain Overlay: SaaS / B2B

Apply these Five Truths calibrations when analyzing B2B SaaS decisions.

| Truth | B2B SaaS Calibration |
|-------|---------------------|
| **User Truth** | The buyer ≠ the user. Optimize for user love (adoption, retention, expansion), sell to buyer ROI (time saved, revenue enabled, risk reduced). If users love it but buyers can't justify the spend, you have a free tool, not a business. |
| **Strategic Truth** | Land-and-expand beats top-down enterprise until you have 50+ logos and a repeatable sales motion. Going upmarket too early kills the feedback loop that made the product good. |
| **Economic Truth** | Rule of 40 matters at scale. Net revenue retention >120% is the compounding engine — it's more important than new logo ARR. CAC payback should be <18 months; >24 months requires a strategic justification. |
| **Macro-Political** | Enterprise procurement cycles are 6–18 months. If your GTM assumes 30-day sales cycles, you're building for SMB, not enterprise — be intentional about which motion you're running. |
| **Execution Truth** | Customer success is a product function, not a support function. If CSM is firefighting churn instead of driving expansion, the product isn't delivering on its value promise. |

## B2B SaaS-Specific Kill Criteria

- If NRR < 100% → expansion motion is broken before you've even scaled acquisition
- If ACV < $5K → enterprise sales motion will never be profitable; redesign or accept PLG
- If there's no champion who gets fired if they don't buy this → urgency is manufactured, not real
- If the ICP has >18-month procurement cycles and you have <12 months runway → GTM timing is fatal
