# Mode: `narrative` — Full Template

**Stance:** Category architect. The goal is not to describe the product — it's to define the frame through which the market will judge it.

**Use when:** Positioning is vague or generic. Competing products are defined by a category you didn't create. The pitch works internally but lands flat externally. Fundraising narrative is weak.

**Do NOT use when:** The product is pre-PMF and the story doesn't yet have evidence — you'll be writing fiction. Validate the insight first.

---

## Required Outputs

- [ ] Status quo frame written from inside the customer's reality (not solution frame)
- [ ] Non-obvious insight named (the thing the market hasn't absorbed)
- [ ] Category claim defined ("not a better X" — a new frame you own)
- [ ] Narrative verdict (Ownable / Borrowed / Fragile / Unearned)
- [ ] The one phrase anchoring every external conversation

---

## Output Template

```
## Narrative Architecture: [Product/Company Name]

### Status Quo Frame
What is the world the customer lives in today? What does this problem look like from inside their reality — not your solution frame?

### The Insight
The non-obvious truth about the market, customer, or technology that the category hasn't absorbed yet. This is what makes the narrative earned, not manufactured.

### Category Claim
[Product] is the [category definition]. Not "a better [existing thing]" — a new frame that you own.
- The old world: [what existed before]
- The new world: [what you're creating]
- Why now: [what changed to make this possible today and not 3 years ago]

### Narrative Arc
One paragraph. No jargon. A first-principles description of what you do, for whom, and why it matters — written as if explaining to an intelligent outsider who has never seen this space.

### Proof Points
The 3 pieces of evidence that make the narrative credible — not aspirational but already true:
1. [Evidence]
2. [Evidence]
3. [Evidence]

### Narrative Risks
| Risk | Why it undermines the narrative | Counter-move |
|------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| [Competitor reframe] | [why] | [response] |
| [Market doesn't believe the insight] | [why] | [response] |
| [Category too new / too familiar] | [why] | [response] |

### Recommendation
**Narrative verdict:** [Ownable / Borrowed / Fragile / Unearned]
**The one phrase that should anchor every external conversation:** [phrase]
**What to stop saying immediately:** [the framing that undercuts the narrative]
```
