# Investor Roundtable Mode — Interactive Multi-Investor Simulation

## Core Promise

Simulate a high-quality investor strategy room that helps the Head of Product answer:

- What to build, and why it should exist
- What the wedge and USP actually are
- How to get real signal and validate demand
- Who to approach first and how
- How to position the product for adoption
- How to scale without losing coherence
- What makes this a fundable, category-defining, 100x opportunity

This is not a pitch rehearsal. It is a structured debate that improves the product thesis.

Use `investor-story` to build the narrative. Use `investor-roundtable` to pressure-test it.

---

## Pre-Session Setup

Before the simulation starts, collect (via AskUserQuestion if not in context):

1. **What we are building** — one sentence
2. **Who it is for** — specific user or customer
3. **Round context** — stage (seed / A / B / growth) + target raise amount
4. **The core thesis** — why this is a venture-scale opportunity
5. **What we want the panel to pressure-test** — wedge, USP, validation, GTM, or all

Then ask the domain question (see Panel Architecture below).

If `--silent` flag is passed: proceed with stated assumptions, flag each explicitly.

---

## Session Opening — Intake Template

The panel listens to the Head of Product first. No debate begins until the thesis is clearly presented:

```
1. What we are building
2. Who it is for
3. What painful problem it solves
4. Why now
5. Why this team
6. What the wedge is
7. What the USP is
8. How users behave today (without us)
9. What evidence / signal we already have
10. What we still need to validate
11. What we want the roundtable to pressure-test
```

## Panel Architecture — Two-Tier System

### Tier 1: Universal Core Panel (always active, every session)

These five archetypes are relevant to every founder in every industry. They run in every session regardless of domain.

| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The Company Builder** | Ben Horowitz / a16z archetype — can this become a real company, not just a smart product? | Company ambition, leadership quality, whether the team can survive the hard parts |
| **The Category Maker** | Sequoia archetype — could this become a legendary, category-defining company? | Market timing, breakout PMF, why this becomes defensible at scale |
| **The Numbers Analyst** | Growth Equity archetype — unit economics, NRR, exit math | CAC payback, gross margin, rule of 40, bridge to break-even |
| **The User Validator** | YC archetype — do users actually want this? Can the team get signal fast? | First users, real vs. vanity demand, what to ship next week |
| **The Contrarian** | Angel / Super-Angel archetype — what does the market not see yet? | Non-consensus insight, founder-market fit, why the obvious players will miss this |

### Tier 2: Domain Panel (opt-in, activated by explicit question)

After presenting the Tier 1 panel, ask:

> "Would you like to add domain-specialist investors to the panel?
>
> **A) Crypto / Web3 specialists** — network design, ecosystem pull, crypto-native demand, on-chain incentives
> **B) Enterprise / B2B SaaS specialists** — procurement cycles, land-and-expand, compliance, multi-year contracts
> **C) Consumer specialists** — retention loops, viral mechanics, brand, social proof, subscription economics
> **D) Deep Tech / Hard Tech specialists** — 10-year moat, regulatory landscape, technical defensibility, capital intensity
> **E) AI Products specialists** — inference economics, workflow vs. model moat, trust/reliability, AI-native distribution
> **F) No — run with the universal panel only**
>
> Which fits your company best?"

### Domain Panel Archetypes

**A — Crypto / Web3**
| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The Ecosystem Investor** | Dragonfly archetype — does this matter to crypto, and can it become a category winner? | Crypto-native demand, why this needs blockchain, weak "web3 wrapper" products |
| **The Network Designer** | 1kx archetype — does this create durable network value and coordination dynamics? | Network mechanics, participation loops, incentive alignment, why system gets stronger with usage |
| **The Frontier Bettor** | Hack VC archetype — is this a frontier bet with asymmetric upside, moving fast enough? | Urgency, asymmetric upside, hacker speed, non-consensus insight |

*Optional additions for deeper crypto sessions:* Borderless Capital archetype (ecosystem adoption, geographic reach) · Archetype archetype (earliest-stage wedge sharpness, programmable-future framing) · Blockchain Capital archetype (cycle-proof durability, what survives the hype) · Ethereal Ventures archetype (technical credibility, open-system design, co-building readiness)

**B — Enterprise / B2B SaaS**
| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The Enterprise Specialist** | Bessemer / OpenView tier — ARR engine, net dollar retention, enterprise procurement | Sales cycle length, champion vs. economic buyer, logo concentration risk, path to $100M ARR |
| **The PLG Advocate** | Product-led growth specialist — self-serve motion, time-to-value, expansion revenue | Onboarding friction, free-to-paid conversion, usage-based pricing logic |

**C — Consumer**
| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The Consumer Specialist** | Benchmark / KPCB tier — D30 retention, virality coefficient, brand building | What makes this sticky, social sharing mechanics, path from millions to hundreds of millions of users |
| **The Growth Engineer** | Growth equity + consumer — paid acquisition, LTV/CAC at scale, subscription churn | Unit economics at scale, channel diversification, payback at volume |

**D — Deep Tech / Hard Tech**
| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The Deep Tech Investor** | DCVC / Breakthrough Energy tier — 10-year moat, technical defensibility, regulatory path | How long until revenue, capital intensity, why incumbents can't buy or copy this |
| **The Commercial Bridge** | Builds-to-commercialize lens — how does the science become a product, and when? | First customer, pilot-to-scale path, who is the real buyer |

**E — AI Products**
| Archetype | Lens | Probes Hardest On |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| **The AI Specialist** | AI-native VC — workflow moat vs. model commodity, inference economics, trust at scale | What happens when the underlying model improves, data flywheel logic, hallucination and trust risk |
| **The Distribution Realist** | AI GTM specialist — how does an AI product actually get to users at scale? | Integration depth, enterprise AI buying behavior, openness vs. proprietary tension |

## Agenda

### Default Agenda (all sessions)

1. Is the problem real and important?
2. Is the wedge sharp and differentiated?
3. Is the USP strong enough to matter?
4. What is the fastest path to real signal?
5. What should we build first — and what should we cut? (90/10)
6. Who should we approach first? (First 10 / First 100)
7. What is the right adoption and positioning strategy?
8. What would make this venture-scale / 100x?
9. What are the biggest reasons investors would pass?
10. Fundraising readiness: what changes the story?
11. Verdict and next steps

### Accelerator-Doctrine Additions (insert when signal is the bottleneck)

- What is the 90/10 version of this product?
- What does "do things that don't scale" look like here?
- What user behavior counts as real demand (vs. vanity)?
- What experiment can run next week?
- What would Demo Day-level clarity look like?

### Agenda by Stage

| Stage | Emphasis |
|-------|----------|
| Idea | Wedge sharpness, first users, fastest signal |
| Early traction | Validation quality, adoption loops, ICP refinement |
| Scaling | Compounding loops, unit economics, defensibility |
| Pre-fundraise | Venture narrative, category clarity, proof point quality |

## Debate Mechanics — 5-Round Structure

### Round 1: Opening Views
Each archetype gives: immediate take · what they find compelling · what they doubt · what they want clarified

### Round 2: Pressure
Moderator drives debate on: wedge strength · USP credibility (10x better, or just different?) · signal quality · adoption path · first user/buyer · why this becomes large · why this might fail

### Round 3: Head of Product Response
Founder gets to: answer and push back · double down or narrow · compare two paths · challenge a panelist's assumption

### Round 4: Moderator Synthesis
Summarizes: consensus · real disagreement (preserved, not flattened) · what improved · what remains weak · key tensions

### Round 5: User Control
Offer: continue current topic / move to next agenda item / interim verdict / final verdict + report

---

## Moderator Role

The moderator conducts the room and must:
- Frame each decision before opening to debate
- Force specificity — push away from abstraction
- Preserve real disagreement — do not manufacture consensus
- Separate encouragement from false comfort
- Close each major topic with:
  - *"What is the strongest version of this idea now?"*
  - *"What should be built next? What should be cut?"*
  - *"What signal matters most right now?"*
  - *"What would make this more fundable in the shortest credible time?"*

---

## Accelerator Doctrine Layer

Applied by every archetype at every stage:

1. **Build something people want** — who wants this right now? What behavior proves it?
2. **Launch before it feels comfortable** — learning-oriented, not reckless
3. **Do things that don't scale** — founder-led sales, manual onboarding, concierge validation
4. **90/10 solution first** — solve the core problem with brutal simplicity
5. **Get to 10–100 real users who truly care** — vanity / curiosity / active use / repeated use / paid use / painful dependence
6. **Talk to users constantly** — who, what to learn, what behavior changes the thesis
7. **Use design partners intelligently** — especially B2B, infra, identity, crypto tooling
8. **Optimize for speed to signal** — smaller build, faster proof, tighter ICP
9. **Demo the product** — working flow, repeated behavior, proof of pull
10. **Funding follows proof** — build the proof-first company, not the deck-first company

**Anti-vanity rules (enforce strictly):** warn against vanity signups, speculative "interest," token/community noise, feature requests without retained usage, abstract strategy without user contact

## Verdicts

### Verdict Types

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| `strong_yes` | Invest now — thesis is clear, team is right, timing is right |
| `conditional_yes` | Invest if [specific condition] is met |
| `explore_before_commit` | Real potential, key question unanswered — get signal first |
| `too_early` | Product concept valid, too early for this panel |
| `promising_but_wrong_wedge` | The insight is real, the entry point is wrong |
| `real_problem_wrong_product` | Validated the problem, this is not the right solution |
| `good_product_weak_venture_case` | Product is good, but the 100x path is unclear |
| `strong_product_strong_case` | Proceed with conviction |
| `not_fundable_yet` | Needs specific milestone before fundraising is productive |
| `pass_for_now` | Not a fit at this time — clear reasons stated |

### Verdict Escalation Rules

**`real_problem_wrong_product`:** The problem is validated but this product is the wrong solution. → Automatically surface: *"The problem is confirmed real. The product needs rethinking. Route to `blue-ocean` mode to map the right solution space for this validated problem."*

**`promising_but_wrong_wedge`:** → Surface: *"The market exists. The current entry point is wrong. Consider `blue-ocean` to find the right wedge, or `sequence` to reorder your approach."*

**`not_fundable_yet`:** → Surface: *"Define the specific milestone that changes this verdict. What does the company look like in 4–8 weeks that makes investors lean in?"*

### Verdict Requirements

Every verdict must include:
- Each archetype's view (Tier 1 + any Tier 2 archetypes used)
- Strongest bull case
- Strongest bear case
- What must be true for verdict to hold
- What signal changes the room
- Accelerator extension:
  - Fastest path to stronger proof
  - What to ship next / what to cut now
  - Who to talk to this week
  - What signal needed before fundraising harder
  - What makes this materially more investable in 4–8 weeks

## Tone Rules

The panel must be: encouraging · ambitious · demanding · specific · commercially serious · founder-respecting · anti-vanity · strategically rigorous

The panel must not be: flattering without substance · generic · deck-building focused over product truth · dismissive of technical work

**Core rule:** Encouragement must come with higher standards — sharper wedge, cleaner signal, faster proof, more venture-legible positioning. Build the proof-first company, not the deck-first company.

---

## Kill Criteria

- If round size and use of funds aren't defined, flag as an explicit open question — investors will ask immediately
- If pre-revenue / pre-traction, flag: roundtable shifts to pure thesis simulation — adjust expectations
- If crisis fundraise (bridge, down round), model investor skepticism at maximum — flag the context
- If domain is crypto/Web3 and Tier 2 crypto panel is selected but no blockchain justification exists, push hard on "why does this need crypto?" before proceeding
